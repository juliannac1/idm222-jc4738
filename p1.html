Can you hear me?

Sometimes I feel like I can’t catch my breath and I’m spending the whole day just searching for my body’s rhythm. It seems I won’t catch up but my eyes are at the back of my head and I can’t see where I’m going either. I could be walking in a dark alleyway for hours and realize my destination is in the other direction. “Lost” is a layer of stone carved on my forehead, right next to the grave of a stranger I can’t quite remember the name of but she was a sweet girl. A kind, little, sweet girl. She was a body of gold with two gems around the sides of her eyes that glow when she smiles.
A beautiful stranger
A stranger I know everything about


My lips hurt from biting them so much and my scars are itchy again. I have more tears in my eyes than my body can release at a time.
I have bruises for people to see so I can hide the bruises that they can’t. But I can see them. And I cannot seem to run away from them, actually, I even removed my eyes from my face and moved them to my spine. It doesn’t matter the darkest alley I choose to walk through. I can force myself to not see or breathe, but I’ll still feel.
I will still breathe and my chest is still rising when I think it stopped long ago. My bruises began to crack the gold from my stranger and I eventually realized it’s fake after all this time. My tears dimmed the light off her gems.
But it’s okay.
It is okay because she is my stranger. She is mine. She’s me.
Can you hear me?
It is okay, because she is me.
